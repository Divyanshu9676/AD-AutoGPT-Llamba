The Alzheimer's Association offers a 24/7 helpline for support and information on Alzheimer's and dementia, providing resources on understanding the diseases, diagnosis, treatments, research, prevention, and support for caregivers. They also offer information on clinical trials, brain donation, and navigating treatment options. Additionally, the organization provides a comprehensive guide covering topics such as diagnosis, caregiving, safety, legal and financial planning, stages and behaviors of Alzheimer's, caregiver health, resources for specific communities, brain health tips, and research. The guide aims to help individuals affected by Alzheimer's disease navigate its challenges and provide support and information. It also includes information on the IDEAS Study, clinical trials, research grants, professional researchers, and partnerships related to Alzheimer's disease research, as well as details on how to apply for grants, participate in studies, and get involved in research efforts.
