Alzheimer's Disease is a progressive brain disorder that affects memory and cognitive function. It is not a normal part of aging and can be caused by genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. Early diagnosis and treatment can help manage symptoms. Support and resources are available for individuals and families affected by Alzheimer's. The comprehensive guide covers topics such as diagnosis, caregiving, safety, legal and financial planning, clinical trials, stages and behaviors, care options, and resources for specific communities. It also provides information on research efforts, including the IDEAS Study, clinical trials, research grants, and how to get involved in research.
